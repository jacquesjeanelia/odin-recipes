Description

Lasagna is a hearty, layered Italian dish that combines tender sheets of pasta, rich sauces, savory fillings, and gooey cheese into a perfect symphony of flavors. Each bite offers a satisfying balance of textures and tastes, making it a beloved comfort food worldwide.

Traditionally, lasagna features alternating layers of pasta, a robust meat or vegetable-based sauce, creamy ricotta or béchamel, and generous amounts of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese, all baked to golden perfection. The dish can be customized with endless variations, from classic Bolognese-style lasagna to vegetarian versions with roasted vegetables or modern twists like seafood or plant-based options.

Lasagna is not just a meal—it's a centerpiece for family gatherings, celebrations, or cozy dinners. Its make-ahead nature also makes it a practical choice for meal prep or hosting, as the flavors deepen when reheated.

This recipe will guide you through creating a mouthwatering lasagna that will have everyone reaching for seconds!

Ingredients

For the Meat Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound (450g) ground beef
  • 1/2 pound (225g) ground pork
  • 1 can (28 ounces/800g) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Ricotta Mixture

  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried parsley)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Pasta and Cheese Layers

  • 12 lasagna sheets, cooked or no-boil, as preferred
  • 3 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese, for topping

Optional Garnishes

  • Fresh basil leaves, for serving
  • Extra grated Parmesan cheese
